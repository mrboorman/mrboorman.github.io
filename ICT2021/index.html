Computer Lab Rules:

  • COVID, Health & Safety:

    1. Arrive to the classroom with clean hands, and use provided hand sanitizer.
    2. Don't be a space invader!
    3. Wear a mask when in close proximity
    4. Log washroom trips on the provided sheet.
    5. Class-leaving procedure: Log off, then wipe down your station (keyboard, mouse, and all contacted hard surfaces) before you are dismissed.
    6. Know your school in-and-out doors. Don't linger in hallways.

  • General Conduct:

    1. Respect the people, the room, and the equipment in the lab.
    2. Before the bell: arrive be quietly seated and log in.
    3. When asked for attention, give your full attention.
    4. Students are expected to conduct themselves in a cordial, respectful manner that is conducive to their own and all members of the school community's well-being, functioning, and learning. This includes in-person and while using technology at school, home, and all other places.

  • Technology/Lab Use:

    1. No drinks nor food allowed in the lab near the computers.
    2. Check daily if all equipment at your station is working properly and clean. Immediately Report any problems with your station/equipment to the teacher.
    3. Cell phones and electronics (including airpods, etc) are to be OFF AND AWAY according to school policy.
    4. The lab computers are to be used for school work ONLY.
    5. Save your files regularly on your H:\ drive only.
    6. Log off your computer (and wipe it down) before you leave.
    7. Don't communicate with Mr. Boorman about marks/grades digitally (email, classroom comments or otherwise). Marks conversations are face-to-face only.

  • Attendance, Absences, Material Covered & Assignment Due Dates:

    1. In-class attendance is mandatory. A lot of material is covered each day. You are responsible for learning the material missed in class. Use Google classroom postings as a starting point.
    2. Miss a class? rule-of-thumb: Bring a parent/guardian note the next day.
    3. Assignments have expected due dates, but Mr. Boorman is generally very flexible. Most assignments can be handed in late without penalty, ERXCEPT: time-sensitive quizzes, tests, presentations, group participation, etc.
    4. Students with a mark of less than 50% at the end of the term/quarter will recieve an F. Mr. Boorman will notify parents/guardians of students who are missing several assignments before an F is assigned.
**Violation of Computer Lab Rules will result in all your school accounts being locked & loss of computer privileges.
